NLP State-of-the-Art

Description

While researching the state-of-the-art for common NLP tasks like text classification I studied and implemented various different pre-trained embeddings, with the goal of being able to compare their performance over very small or unbalanced datasets.

An example of a token-level embedding is BERT, which architecture is shown in the image and which in my studies I've tested with several datasets and setups.

While attempting to find the best model for the task, I wrote various Python Notebooks that implement many different embeddings or language models, they are: Word2Vec, FastText, InferSent, Universal Sentence Encoder, GPT-2, and ELMo. I also tried to apply different architectures like LSTM, Hierarchical Attention Network and Deep Averaging Network, while most of the embeddings only needed a simple linear or logistic regression to produce good results.

This research happened for a period of almost one full year.
